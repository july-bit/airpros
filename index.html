intro

AirPros is an Android application that aims to measure and display the user's environment's current Air Quality index, as well as information on the current humidity and temperature parameters. AirPros is powered by two sensors via the Arduino's circuit board: a MQ-135 for AQI and a DHT-11 for temperature and humidity. Then, using the HC-06 bluetooth module, the data collected by these sensors is transmitted through Bluetooth.

AirPros' mission is not simply to educate citizens, but to inspire and motivate them to act and be a part of the solution.

A I R Q U A L I T Y

1. AQI Index

The AQI index is the de facto global standard for reporting air quality levels. It is commonly used as a standard method for expressing how polluted or clean the air is by measuring five fundamental pollutants: ozone, particle pollution, carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide, and sulfur dioxide. These metrics are then fed into an algorithm, which produces the AQI index. This measure has a scale of 0 to 500 with six subcategories: Good, Moderate, Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups, Unhealthy, Very Unhealthy, and Hazardous.

2. Case study: Bangladesh

Bangladesh, with an average of 71 parts per million (ppm) in 2020, and Pakistan, with an average of 59 ppm, are the world's most polluted countries, according to IQAir's world API ranking report. More astoundingly, in 2013, Sekolah Menengah Teknik Muar, Johor, measured a world-record 663 AQI index.

3. Vietnam's current pollution level

With a comprehensive view on the current status of pollution in the world, we can see how pollution is wreaking havoc on even affluent countries. Vietnam is no exception. Vietnam was among the world's 21 most polluted countries in 2020, according to (https://www.iqair.com/vietnam). Specifically, Vietnam's PM2.5 concentration is 5.6 times the WHO-recommended average air quality index.

4. What can you do?

With rising pollution levels, it is critical that Vietnamese individuals play a greater role in preserving and enhancing the nation's environmental state. Each person may contribute to this objective by taking three simple steps: investing in environmentally friendly items, cultivating a habit of tree planting, and adhering to the three R's - Reduce, reuse, and recycle. Additionally, Vietnamese residents should avoid using plastic or polyethylene bags whenever feasible, as the chemical does not biodegrade. Rather than that, they decompose into tiny harmful fragments, poisoning the soil and contributing to global warming.
